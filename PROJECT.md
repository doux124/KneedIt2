# Kneedit — Project Summary

> Source: [cl.pdf](cl.pdf) (Venture Summary, Proposal, Business Model, Team). Summarized 2026-06-07.

## One-liner
**Kneedit is a smart knee sleeve that prevents and monitors knee osteoarthritis (OA) in elderly Singaporeans** — turning everyday wear into a passive health assessment, and screen time into guided exercise.

## The Problem
- Knee OA affects an estimated **40% of Singaporeans above 70**; it is progressive, painful, expensive — yet largely **preventable through exercise**.
- The barrier is **adherence, not awareness**. Only **24% of Singaporeans aged 60–74** do regular leisure-time exercise (lowest of any age group).
- Root causes (from stakeholder interviews): fear of injury, social embarrassment, loss of exercise companions, no home accountability.
- Existing products fall short: braces are passive; smartwatches track steps not knee-specific movement; clinical devices (e.g. Kinexcs) are post-surgical, expensive, hospital-bound.

## The Product (three layers)
1. **Smart sleeve** — clinical-grade, knee-specific sensing: kinematic (range of motion), electromyographic (muscle engagement), photoplethysmographic (tissue health). Built for elderly: no setup, no screen interaction, on-sleeve **LED strip** for instant feedback, feels like a normal compression sleeve.
2. **Behavioural layer (companion app)** — monitors daily screen time; past a threshold it **locks the phone** and shows a guided, clinically-validated exercise session (seated knee extensions, ankle circles, quad sets). The phone **won't unlock until the sleeve verifies genuine completion**. "No willpower required."
3. **Caregiver ecosystem** — dashboard for nominated family members: real-time exercise history, ROM trends, and **automatic alerts** for missed sessions or mobility decline. Caregivers configure thresholds on the elder's behalf.

## Differentiation
The only product combining **clinical-grade knee monitoring + a behavioural adherence mechanism + a caregiver dashboard** in a consumer-priced everyday wearable.

## Business Model
- **Direct-to-consumer hardware** — the sleeve is the paid product (primary revenue).
- **Companion app is free** — maximises adoption (freemium: free behavioural layer, premium clinical monitoring).
- Future revenue: partnerships (ActiveSG, NTUC Health), institutional B2B (eldercare facilities), anonymised population mobility data for insurers/healthcare.

## Stage & Roadmap
Currently **Idea & Concept** stage with a working app interface built at NUS. Next: prototype funding → clinical validation of sensor accuracy → elderly user testing → HSA medical-device regulatory pathway → pilot → launch & scale (Singapore first, then Malaysia/Thailand/Japan).

## Team
Undergraduate students at the **National University of Singapore**: Advait, Jordan, Brigitte, En Xi. Expertise across biomedical engineering, software development, and UX. Built ground-up from direct interviews with elderly users and caregivers.
